03 — Voice & Tone
Confident

Expert, never clinical.

Lauren Bays Beauty speaks with the authority of someone who has seen thousands of clients — but never talks down to them. Use precise language that builds trust without jargon overload.

"BBL targets the root cause of sun damage and redness — not just the surface."
Warm

Inviting, not salesy.

The brand feels like a trusted friend who happens to be a skincare expert. Copy should feel like a conversation — direct, caring, and always focused on the client's outcome.

"Not sure where to start? That's exactly what your free Skin Analysis is for."
Aspirational

Results-driven, not promise-heavy.

Speak to transformation without overpromising. Lead with real outcomes — glow, confidence, consistency — over superlatives. Let the results do the talking.

"Clients notice the difference after one session. Lasting change builds over time."
Refined

Luxe without the pretension.

The brand sits at the intersection of luxury and approachability. Copy is polished and intentional — never stiff, never casual to the point of cheapening the experience.

"This is your hour. We'll handle everything else."
04 — Visual Do's & Don'ts
Do
Use warm, soft-lit photography on neutral cream or linen backgrounds
Feature close-up skin and treatment shots that show texture and glow
Leave generous white space — the brand breathes
Use Cormorant Garamond in italic for editorial elegance
Keep copy concise, benefit-led, and client-focused
Use the espresso + cream combination as the default high-contrast pairing
Don't
Don't use cool, blue-toned or overly bright white backgrounds
Don't use clinical before/after imagery without proper framing and context
Don't clutter layouts — one focal point per section
Don't use more than two typefaces or introduce new fonts
Don't use hype language — words like "revolutionary," "miracle," or "instant results"
Don't stretch, recolor, or alter the logo in any way
